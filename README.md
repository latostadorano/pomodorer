# POMODORER

There is a version of you that knows exactly what time it is. You are probably not that version right now, and that is okay, that is actually why you are here.

---

## What this is

Pomodorer is a timer. It sits on your screen and it watches the time so that you don't have to. You have other things to do. Important things. Things that require you to go somewhere inside yourself that doesn't have clocks.

I thought about what it feels like to look up from something you love — a problem, a drawing, a sentence that isn't working yet — and realize that four hours have passed and your coffee is cold and the light in the room has changed completely without your permission. I made this for that feeling. Not to prevent it. Just to put a small, quiet thing next to it.

---

## What it does

It counts. It glows a little. When it's done it makes a sound — not a loud sound, more like someone touching your shoulder very gently, the way a considerate person would.

You can run more than one timer at once, if you are the kind of person who is doing more than one thing at once, which you probably are. Each one gets its own color. Red, blue, yellow. They breathe.

There is a mode where the timer lives as a thin line at the edge of your screen. A strip of light. You barely see it. But it's there, and you know it's there, and somehow that is enough.

---

## How to use it

Open it. Type how long you want to focus — `25m`, `1h`, `45m`, whatever feels true right now. Type how long you want to rest. Press START, or just press Enter, which feels more like a beginning somehow.

Then go. Go do the thing. The timer will hold the time for you. When the focus is over it will tell you. When the rest is over it will tell you again. You decide what to do with that information.

That's all. That is genuinely all.

---

## Web version

Open `pomodorer_web/pomodorer_10.html` in your browser. Nothing to install. It's already there.

## Mac app

Open the `.dmg`. Drag it to Applications. Open it. There it is.

## Who this is for

It's for you, specifically. I don't know your name but I know that sometimes you lose track of time in a way that feels like a superpower and a problem at the same time. I know you've opened a document at noon and emerged at dusk slightly confused about what happened to the afternoon, but also — the thing got done. The thing is better now. You went somewhere and you came back.

Pomodorer doesn't try to fix that. It just stands at the door and keeps a light on.
